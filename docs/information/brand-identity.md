> 2. How we build and what we do differently

**Business first, code second.**
We don't open an editor until we understand how your business actually runs and the underlying problem. Half the value is figuring out what to build, not building it.

**You own everything.**
No subscriptions. No lock-in. Every tool we deliver is yours to keep, modify, or shut off. You get the code.

**Measured, not promised.**
Every tool ships with analytics built in. You see exactly what it's saving you, in hours, dollars, or fewer mistakes.

*What you won't get: account managers, status decks, or a sales team you've never met. You talk to the person building your tool — every step.*

### A process, not a guess.
Rooted in engineering principles
*Five stages from idea to running tool. You see — and approve — the work at every step.*

| Stage | What it is | What you get |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Discover** *(week 1)* | We learn how your business runs day-to-day and find where time is leaking. | A written list of opportunities, ranked by impact vs. effort. |
| **2. Design the workflow** *(week 1–2)* | We map what the tool will do, with words and diagrams. No code yet. | A clear plan, in your language, that you sign off on. |
| **3. Detailed plan** *(week 2)* | We spec out data, integrations, and edge cases. Every "what about when..." gets answered. | A build plan with no mid-project surprises. |
| **4. Prototype** *(week 2–4)* | A working version, fast, using your real data. You use it in your real workflow. | Something running, not slides. |
| **5. Refine & hand off** *(week 4–6)* | We tune the tool against how it's actually used, train your team, and hand over the docs. | A tool your team owns — plus everything needed to keep it running without us. |

*Larger transformations follow the same 5 stages over 4–6 months.*

## Synthesis Notes

### Color palette direction

> **"Atlantic Soft"** — soft blue + warm white base, with a warm amber accent to resolve the tension between *fun & inviting* and *professional*. Avoids generic SaaS blue and pure black/white harshness.

| Role | Hex | Use |
|------|-----|-----|
| Primary Blue | `#5B95C9` | Brand color, logo, headers, hero blocks |
| Deep Ink | `#202A38` | Body text, dark backgrounds, emphasis |
| Warm White | `#FAFBFC` | Main background |
| Warm Accent | `#E8965A` | CTAs, links, highlights — the "fun" |
| Slate Neutral | `#6B7785` | Secondary text, borders, captions |

**60-30-10 application:**
- 60% Warm white background
- 30% Soft blue (sections, illustrations, headers)
- 10% Warm amber (CTAs, accents only)

**Logic:**
- Soft blue (`#5B95C9`) — approachable, touch of teal warmth, not corporate SaaS blue
- Warm amber (`#E8965A`) — single warm accent resolves the cool-palette sterility, echoes Anthropic's warm tones
- Warm white (`#FAFBFC`) — softer than pure white, easier reading
- Deep navy (`#202A38`) instead of black — matches "no sharp UI" rule, passes AAA accessibility
